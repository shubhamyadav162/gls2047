ABOUT P anIIT 
Pan IIT Alumni India (PanIIT) is an umbrella organization, representing alumni of all Indian 
Institutes of Technology (IITs). Pan IIT is a not-for-profit Society under the Societies Registration 
Act XXI of 1860 with Registration No. S-56680 of 2006 dated 20 September 2006 duly registered 
with the Registrar of Societies, Government of NCT of Delhi, Delhi. It is the Indian legal entity of 
the global Pan IIT Alumni movement.
The movement, over the years, has developed and and spread in various parts of the world.  
Besides India, it has a very active presence in the United States, Canada, United Kingdom, 
Korea, Singapore, and Australia.
PanIIT is the global body of IIT alumni, representing 5,00,000+ IITians from India’s most 
prestigious institutions
A driving force in leadership, entrepreneurship, and national growth
IIT alumni today are Global CEOs, Unicorn founders, Venture Capital Partners, and Policymakers

TECHNOLOGY | INVESTMENTS I GOVERNANCE I DIPLOMACY 
IIT Alumni are a powerhouse in India’s startup ecosystem. Out of 108 unicorns, 68 have at 
least one IIT graduate behind them—think Flipkart or Paytm. IIT founders have attracted 
nearly half of India’s total equity funding in the past decade, driving fintech and deep-tech 
growth. With strong problem-solving skills and global networks, many are now shifting to the 
investor side as angels and VCs, pumping billions back into the ecosystem.
VC trends in India are also heating up. Funding is rebounding sharply—October alone saw 
$5.3B across 100 deals, up 81% year-on-year. H1 hit $5.7B, led by global players like Sequoia and 
Tiger. InvestmentsIITians have been pivotal contributors in this technological evolution across the world. From 
founding groundbreaking startups to leading R&D at Fortune 500 companies, IIT alumni 
consistently push the boundaries of what is possible. Their work in AI, semiconductor design, 
fintech, climate tech, and space engineering has influenced global standards and accelerated 
progress in multiple sectors. IITians also play key roles in government, policy-making, and 
academia, shaping technology frameworks that impact millions.Technology
Strong governance is a growth accelerator. Stable policies, low corruption, and faster 
bureaucracy have helped India shift from a “stalling” to a “scaling” economy. Reforms like GST, 
the Insolvency Code, and major digital infrastructure upgrades have improved India’s Ease of 
Doing Business by 29 ranks since 2016. Better courts and dispute resolution cut economic drag 
by 3.5% last year, while stronger institutions pulled in $21B in FDI, boosting manufacturing and 
jobs. Challenges remain—land laws and red tape still slow infrastructure—but good governance 
consistently turns disorder into economic opportunity.
Digital infrastructure shows how governance pays off. UPI now processes 12 billion 
transactions a month, powering small-business growth. Aadhaar’s 920 million verified IDs 
reduce fraud and speed up welfare delivery.Governance
Diplomacy is powering India’s economic rise. Big deals like the India-EFTA pact and the US 
iCET partnership are unlocking investments, boosting chip and AI collaborations, and pushing 
FDI up 18% to $35B. Platforms like the Quad and G20 are strengthening supply chains and 
easing global market access, while smoother visas help India pull top talent. Geopolitical bumps 
remain, but overall, foreign policy is turning India into a magnet for capital and innovation.
Trade agreements amplify the momentum. India now has 13 FTAs and 6 PTAs, driving growth 
with neighbors like Bhutan, Nepal, and Bangladesh through low- or zero-tariff access. Diplomacy
